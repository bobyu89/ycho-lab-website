About

Assistant Professor in the Department of Nursing at National Defense Medical University and Director of the Smart Health Translation & Innovation Lab. His research focuses on anhedonia and depression-risk monitoring, ecological momentary assessment (EMA), moving-average personalized dynamic monitoring, simulation-based nursing education, and AI-assisted nursing training — with a commitment to moving research from papers into field validation and clinical workflows.

Research Philosophy

We care not only about model performance, but about how models are understood, adopted, and used by clinicians to improve care decisions — rooted in nursing science, powered by digital measurement, and validated in clinical and educational settings, building explainable, deployable, and scalable smart-health and mental-health innovation.
